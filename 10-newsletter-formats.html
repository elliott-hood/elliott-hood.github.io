1

Briefs and Bullets

Examples: Morning Brew, The Hustle, 1440, Front Office Sports, Stacked Marketer
The most popular newsletter format. Pioneered by Morning Brew & The Hustle.
Two main sections:
  • Briefs: 3-5 short stories (top story 300 words, others 150-250 words)
  • Bullets: Quick summaries, fun links, useful resources
Use if:

Your newsletter covers news and current events. Target: under 5 minutes to read.

Don't use if: You focus on deep analysis, education only, or curation only.
2

TLDR Summaries

Examples: TLDR Newsletter (1.2M subs, 40% open rate), Growth Daily, ELEVATOR
Link to article in headline ‚Üí Summarize below. Organized by sections (Big Tech, Science, etc.)
Growth Daily's upgrade:
  • Bullet 1: Summary of the article
  • Bullet 2: Actionable takeaway + insights
Use if:

You focus on a niche or industry vertical. Your curated links and summaries are hyper-relevant.

Critical: Curation quality makes or breaks this format. Poor curation = high churn.
3

All Bullets

Examples: Axios, Exec Sum (200k subs, 80%+ bullets), Ben's Bites, Quartz Daily Brief
100+ bullets per issue. Easy to skim and find relevant information quickly.
Use if:

Your audience are professionals who need daily summaries for their careers. Publish daily or 3-4x/week.

Best niches:
  • Finance & investment banking
  • Venture capital
  • Crypto
  • Private equity
  • Politics
Exec Sum has one of the highest open rates ever tracked
4

Curated List

Examples: 5 Bullet Friday (Tim Ferriss), 3-2-1 Newsletter (James Clear), Marketing Examples
Numbered template that writer fills in consistently.
Examples of templates:
  • Tim: 5 things I've loved/used/read
  • James: 3 ideas + 2 quotes + 1 question
  • Harry: 3 examples + 2 tips + 1 tweet
Use if:

You're an expert, thought leader, or brand. Very easy to write IF curation is good.

Requirement: People need to care about YOUR opinions and curations. Build trust with original content first.
5

High-Value Curations

Examples: Creator Wizard, Who Sponsors Stuff, Scott's Cheap Flights, The Offer Sheet
Research + expertise to find highly valuable curations that make/save readers money.
What they curate:
  • Sponsorship opportunities for creators
  • Budget flight deals
  • Real estate investment opportunities
  • Content that requires time, expertise, or unique data to find
Use if:

You can find things that make people money, save them money, or significantly improve their lives.

üí∞ Easy to monetize: Readers willing to pay. Often 20-30% free curations, rest behind paywall.
6

Daily Analysis

Examples: Stratechery (tech), Milk Road (crypto), Van Trump Report (agriculture)
Daily deep analysis of an industry. Pioneered by Stratechery in 2013.
Use if:

You're an expert who can write deep daily analysis. High skill and drive required.

Van Trump: ~$20M/year in paid subs
Stratechery: $3M+/year (26k+ paid @ $120/year)
Not for most people: Requires daily expert-level analysis. But extremely lucrative if you can do it.
7

Weekly Deep Dives

Examples: The Generalist, Marketing Examined, Growth In Reverse, Houck's Newsletter
Weekly deep dive on one topic. Unlimited niches possible.
Topic examples:
  • Tech/crypto/AI trends analysis
  • How top creators grow
  • Marketing case studies
  • Startup growth tactics
Use if:

Great for new creators or brands wanting to grow organically and establish expertise.

‚úÖ Benefits: Deep dives get shared more than curated content. Easy to repurpose into social media.
8

Visual Newsletters

Examples: Chartr (400k subs), Growth Design, Visual Capitalist, Bay Area Times
Data ‚Üí infographics and charts. Visual-first with "brief" style copy below images.
Use if:

You have the skill to make beautiful and useful infographics.

üí° Alternative: Include ONE visual/graph/chart as a section. Much easier than creating 3+ visuals per issue.
Expect more visual newsletters in various niches soon
9

ML/Algorithm Curation

Examples: Refind, Essentials
Algorithms analyze thousands of articles ‚Üí Share best ones tailored to user's selected interests.
‚ö†Ô∏è Probably not for you: Competing with other newsletters AND aggregators like Apple News and Google News. Very tough to get right.
10

Short and Daily

Examples: A.Word.A.Day (400k subs, since 2002), Get 8 AM
Ultra-simple daily concept. A.Word.A.Day: literally one word per day with meaning, etymology, info.
Use if:

You have a unique angle for simple daily content. Lots of possibilities.

Trade-off: Because content is short and simple, growing and monetizing may be much harder.

Quick Format Selection Guide

News & Current Events: Briefs & Bullets, TLDR Summaries, All Bullets
Building Authority: Weekly Deep Dives, Daily Analysis, Visual
Established Expert: Curated List, High-Value Curations
Easy to Monetize: High-Value Curations, Daily Analysis
