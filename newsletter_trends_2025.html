Setting Up DMARC Enforcement
In 2024, Gmail and Yahoo required all newsletter operators (sending 5,000+ emails) to set up three authentication protocols: SPF, DKIM, and DMARC. In 2025, Microsoft joined them. Most people stopped at "none"‚Äîleaving domains vulnerable.
üîê What is DMARC?
Domain-based Message Authentication, Reporting & Conformance. Prevents email spoofing. You tell inboxes where you send email from and what to do if someone tries to spam/spoof/phish using your address.

‚ö†Ô∏è Why This Matters
DMARC set to "none" = compliance with inbox requirements BUT bad actors can still spoof your domain. Enforcement level ("quarantine" or "reject") = actually blocking spoofing.
‚úÖ What To Do
  • 1
    Check your DMARC policy (guide available)
  • 2
    If at "none," change to enforcement ("quarantine" or "reject")
  • 3
    Only applies if sending from custom domain (Beehiiv/Substack handles this for you)
  • 4
    Do this ASAP to protect your domain from spoofing
