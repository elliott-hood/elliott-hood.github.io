How to Build Your Course

Productize the Name

Don't call it a "course" ‚Äî use words that make it feel like a tangible product: Accelerator, Starter Kit, Roadmap, Blueprint, Playbook, Cheatsheet

Start with Content You Already Have

Don't write from scratch. Review your most opened, clicked, and responded-to newsletter issues. Look for natural themes that connect them into a 5-7 email sequence.

Build the Structure

Use "The Crash Course" format ‚Äî covers "everything you need to know" on a topic. Each day is a different high-level guide.

Example Structure: Day-by-Day

  • Day 1: Myths ‚Äî Break misconceptions
  • Day 2: Mistakes ‚Äî Common pitfalls to avoid
  • Day 3: Steps ‚Äî How to get started
  • Day 4: Steps ‚Äî Advanced implementation
  • Day 5: Tips ‚Äî Optimization tactics

Keep Each Lesson Skimmable

Readers are in a hurry. Design for quick consumption with clear takeaways.

  • Keep each day's lesson between 800-1,200 words
  • Use clear subheaders with larger fonts and bolding
  • Mix formatting: bullets, numbers, short paragraphs
  • Add conversational commentary between sections
  • Include visual elements: images, GIFs, infographics
  • Make lessons interactive: videos, polls, Notion workbooks
