1

Wrong Ad Placement in Newsletter

‚ùå The Problem:

Not creating enough breaks in content to insert ads. Treating newsletter like one long block of content = limited ad inventory.

‚úÖ The Solution: Create 3 Ad Tiers

Primary Ads

  • Title/presenting sponsor
  • 150-250 words
  • Includes image

Secondary Ads

  • Native ad or event listings
  • 100-200 words
  • No image

Tertiary Ads

  • Bottom of newsletter
  • Classified ads
  • 25-50 words
Example Structure (Local Newsletter):
Newsletter logo
Primary sponsor logo
Main story
Primary native ad
Additional news
Secondary native ad
Events calendar (with sponsor)
Classified ads
Crossword puzzle
üí° Result: Single newsletter with multiple ad slots = more potential revenue
2

Not Charging Enough

‚ùå The Problem:

Too many newsletters undercharge advertisers. Pricing ads too low from the start.

‚úÖ The Solution: Calculate Using CPM (Cost Per Mille)
CPM Formula: 1. List Size √ó Open Rate = Average Opens 2. Average Opens √∑ 1,000 = Units 3. Units √ó CPM Rate = Ad Price
Example Calculation:

50,000 list √ó 50% open rate = 25,000 opens

25,000 √∑ 1,000 = 25 units

25 √ó $40 CPM = $1,000 per ad

Newsletter Type CPM Range (USD)
Large media publications (wide topics) $5-$25
Local media publications $20-$50
General "need to know" (business news) $25-$60
Niche industry (finance, travel) $25-$60
B2B or career-focused (marketing) $50-$100+
Independent creators/influencers $20-$60
Hobby-centric $20-$30
üí° Rule: The more niche = higher CPM (harder for advertisers to reach). When in doubt, start higher. Even 10k subscribers should charge at least $500 for native ad.
3

Not Aligned on Metrics That Matter

‚ùå The Problem:

Selling based on total list size (even if huge % haven't opened in months). Putting newsletter CTOR in media kit instead of ad CTOR. Not understanding sponsor's specific goals.

‚ö†Ô∏è Warning Example:

"500,000 readers" but only 20% opened/clicked in 90 days

Actual active list: 100,000 readers

‚úÖ The Solution: Align on True Metrics & Goals
  • Use ACTIVE list size (opened/clicked in past 90 days)
  • Show ad CTOR, not newsletter CTOR (clicks on ads vs clicks on stories)
  • Ask sponsors their goal BEFORE campaign
  • Understand what success looks like for THEM
üí° Key: Each sponsor has different goals (awareness vs sales vs signups). Get on same page about success = better post-campaign conversation = renewals.
4

Not Explaining Why Your Audience is Special

‚ùå The Problem:

Decade ago: big engaged list was enough. Now: advertisers want exact readers they're trying to reach. You need data.

‚úÖ The Solution: Collect Audience Data
Questions to Ask Readers:
  • What is your age?
  • What is your gender?
  • Where do you live? (zip code for local, city/country for topic-based)
  • What is your job title or role? (CEO, VP, manager, etc.)
  • What industry do you work in?
  • How much money do you make per year?
  • What are your hobbies or interests?
How to Collect Data:
  • Email platform analytics
  • Click data (what topics readers care about)
  • Third-party tools (Megahit for B2B = scans list, matches LinkedIn)
  • Best method: SURVEY readers directly
üí° Promote surveys: In newsletter, on thank you page after signup, in welcome series. More data = better explanation of why audience is unique.
5

Haven't Figured Out Sales Process

‚ùå The Problem:

No clear game plan for sales calls. Not prepared with ideas. Multiple meetings killing momentum. Saying "I'll follow up to schedule" = easy to ignore.

‚úÖ The Solution: Systematic Sales Approach
Before the Meeting:
  • Come with 3-5 message ideas that resonate with your audience
  • Mock up ad examples featuring their brand
  • Go full "Mad Men" ‚Äî Don Draper shows up with a pitch
During the Meeting:
  • Show results from other advertisers (builds confidence)
  • Share renewal rate (% who come back after first ad)
  • Book the ad on the call if they're ready (avoid multiple meetings)
  • If they need time: Schedule next call BEFORE ending current one
üí° Pro Move: Saying "no" over email is easy. Saying "no" face-to-face is hard. Schedule next meeting on the call = better close rate.
6

Ad Copy Needs Work

‚ùå The Problem:

Using generic marketing copy. Boring images that say "keep scrolling." Weak calls to action like "click here" or "learn more."

‚úÖ The Solution: Personal, Problem-Solving Copy
Writing the Copy:
  • Recommend like you're talking to a friend
  • Tell readers why YOU like this brand
  • Build around a problem the advertiser solved for you
  • Start with the problem ‚Üí show how advertiser solved it
Choosing Images:
  • Boring image = "keep scrolling, nothing to see"
  • Example: Credit card? NOT stock photo of card itself
  • Instead: Family on vacation + copy about frequent flier miles for dream trip
Call to Action:
  • ‚ùå Bad: "Click here" or "Learn more"
  • ‚úÖ Good: "Invest now" or "Start planning for retirement"
  • Describe the action they'll take or result they'll get
üí° Rule: Personal approach > Generic marketing copy. Every time.
7

Not Showing Sponsors True Value

‚ùå The Problem:

Not helping sponsors see the investment. Focusing only on surface metrics. Missing the lifetime value conversation.

‚úÖ The Solution: Walk Through 3 Key Metrics

CAC

Customer Acquisition Cost
Cost to acquire one new customer

CPC

Cost Per Click
What they paid per click to their site

ROAS

Return on Ad Spend
Money made per dollar spent

üí° Lifetime Value Example:

Scenario: Local newsletter partners with dry cleaner

Ad result: Only 3 new customers (doesn't seem like much)

BUT: People stick with dry cleaners for years

Lifetime value per customer: $500/year

Total value: 3 customers √ó $500 = $1,500

Ad cost: Probably much less than $1,500

ROAS: $1 invested ‚Üí $3+ in value

üí° Follow-up Call Strategy: Walk through numbers ‚Üí Ask how ad performed on their end ‚Üí Dig into ROAS ‚Üí Position success around lifetime value ‚Üí Show the investment ‚Üí Get renewals.
