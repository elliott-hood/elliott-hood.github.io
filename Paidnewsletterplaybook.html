The Reality Check

The Paid Writing Fallacy: "If I make my writing paid, people will pay me for it." ‚Üê FALSE
The Stress Test: If people won't read your free stuff, they definitely won't pay for paid stuff.

Key insight: A paid newsletter isn't a one-time sale. It's a recurring repurchase decision. You must re-sell the value every single month.

2 Types That Actually Work

1. Researched Curation

Subscribers pay you for:

  • Labor
  • Research time
  • Pattern recognition
  • Interpretation
The value exchange:

"I give you $X/month. You research this for me and deliver conclusions."

Why it works:

You're selling leveraged time. Topics that change frequently justify higher retention.

2. Original Thinking

Subscribers pay for:

  • Behind-the-scenes expertise
  • Industry insight
  • Access to your brain at scale
The frame:

A paid newsletter = a book that never ends

Why it works:

It's an expertise subscription. People pay to compress their learning curve.

4-Part Checklist

Your topic must pass ALL four criteria:

1 Infinitely Repeatable

The topic must support endless useful issues without repeating yourself.

Ask yourself:
  • "Could I publish weekly for 2 years without repeating?"
  • "Does the category move fast enough to constantly have 'new'?"
Example: Self-publishing "what's new" moves too slowly vs. finance/politics

2 Tangible (Not Intangible)

Don't sell "ideas." Sell "objects."

Sell these:
  • Prompts
  • Templates
  • Reports
  • Case studies
  • Blueprints
Digital info is "air" ‚Äî your job is making it feel like an asset.

3 Objective Outcome (Not Subjective)

Objective outcomes create strong word-of-mouth and message consistency.

Bad (subjective): "Master productivity"
‚Üí 10 people interpret it 10 ways = weak WOM

Good (objective): "Go from project manager to VP ops"
‚Üí Clear, repeatable story = strong WOM

4 Price-Anchored to Upside

Connect subscription price to tangible ROI.

Anchor to:
  • Money gained
  • Money saved
  • Time saved
  • Risk avoided
Don't say: "Pay $20/mo to read my writing." ‚Üí Say: "Save 10 hours/month of research for $20."
