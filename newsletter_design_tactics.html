The Ultra-Minimal Layout
Layout Click-Focused
50 words or less of text, then a single link to their case study with estimated read time. That's it. No intro, no fluff, no additional links.
The Strategy
Give readers only two options: Click the link or close the email. Like the best landing pages, there's no other action to take.
üí° Why It Works
Their click-to-open rate is almost certainly sky-high. When there's nothing else to do, people click. Zero distractions = maximum engagement on the one thing that matters.
üéØ How to Steal This
For your most important emails (product launches, key announcements), try stripping everything down to one compelling CTA. Remove sidebar content, related links, and extra navigation. Force the focus.

Layout
No design elements besides bolding, angle brackets, asterisks, and horizontal lines. Zero images. Just pure text formatted smartly.

üí° Why It Works

üéØ How to Steal This

Fundraising emails feature two different buttons: one for monthly donations (in their signature orange), one for one-time donations (white).

Giant numbers (newsletter always has 5 items) and lots of white space around bullets for desktop. Mobile gets a streamlined one-column treatment.

üí° Why It Works

üéØ How to Steal This

üí° Why It Works
